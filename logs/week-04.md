# Week 4

**Dates:** 06-29 to 07-05

## Goals

- Strengthen the literature justification for commitment.
- Investigate emotional and reinforcing robot-light behaviors.
- Draft commitment dialogue based on prior research.
- Complete Blossom hardware and software onboarding.
- Begin testing communication between Pastoral and Blossom.

## Approach and Implementation

I reviewed research on the foot-in-the-door effect, active choice, implementation intentions, and commitment in human-robot interaction.

Based on this literature, I drafted dialogue asking participants to make an active plan to try another strategy after becoming stuck or answering incorrectly.

I also reviewed literature on robot LEDs and emotional color displays. The findings suggested that lights can communicate positive emotions, but Blossom’s specific ear-light sequence may still need validation.

During Blossom onboarding, I learned how to connect through SSH, activate the Python environment, test the motors, control the ear lights, and test the speaker.

## Results

- Developed a literature-based commitment condition.
- Drafted active and specific commitment dialogue.
- Created a shorter IRB-friendly explanation.
- Identified a possible need to validate the ear-light sequence.
- Completed Blossom hardware and software onboarding.
- Reviewed the Blossom SDK, movement code, and LED controls.

## Notes

The commitment should involve an active response and a specific plan rather than a vague promise.

The ear-light sequence should either be validated with non-roboticists or carefully justified through literature.
